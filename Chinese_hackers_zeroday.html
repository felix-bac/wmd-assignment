Chinese Hackers Exploit Zero-Day Vulnerability Impacting Millions Globally

By Global Cybersecurity Desk

April 30, 2025

A highly sophisticated Chinese state-sponsored cyber hacking group, Volt Typhoon, has exploited a zero-day vulnerability affecting millions of systems worldwide, triggering alarm signals in the cybersecurity world. The vulnerability, CVE-2024-39717, was found in Versa Director, a network management platform widely utilized by internet service providers (ISPs) and managed service providers (MSPs). This assault has struck critical infrastructure such as IT and ISP sectors with at least four U.S.-based organizations and one in India having been targeted as victims.

The Zero-Day Attack Revealed

The threat is high-severity threat and allows attackers with admin rights to upload malicious programs, which drop an in-house web shell named "VersaMem." The advanced malware is employed for plundering plaintext credentials and executing arbitrary code as well as evading detection. According to researchers at Black Lotus Labs, a company owned by Lumen Technologies, the attacks began as early as June 12, 2024, when the first known exploit hit a U.S. ISP. The web shell, disguised as "VersaTest.png," was uploaded to VirusTotal from Singapore on June 7, 2024, and remains undetected by antivirus software, and it is an active threat to unpatched systems.

The Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) added CVE-2024-39717 to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities list, issuing a warning that such vulnerabilities are the normal target of exploit by bad actors. Versa Networks has then released a patch (version 22.1.4) and advised customers that system hardening and firewall best practices should be applied to minimize the threat. Despite this, the breadth of the breach and malware ability to pivot into downstream networks signifies the scale of the attack.

Volt Typhoon's Strategic Motivation

Volt Typhoon, also known by the alias Bronze Silhouette, is most infamous for targeting U.S. critical infrastructure, including energy, defense, and telecommunications industries. The U.S. federal government agencies like the White House and Department of Defense have previously referred to campaigns of the group as pre-emptive attacks to disrupt communications between the U.S. and Asia in potential future wars, particularly in the case of a Chinese invasion of Taiwan. The group's modus operandi is to infect small-office and home-office machines in order to propagate wider attacks, thus Versa Director offers a very attractive target as it manages network configurations.

"So that folks won't perceive this as being telecom-specific but could be generalized to MSPs and ISPs, which are concentration points that provide access to downstream consumers," explained Black Lotus Labs security researcher Mike Horka. The stolen credentials could potentially allow the attackers to gain entry with which to break into client infrastructure via legitimate entry points, expanding the scope of the attack.

A Growing Trend in Zero-Day Exploitation

This attack is part of a greater wave of zero-day attacks, with Google Threat Analysis Group (TAG) and Mandiant stating that there were 97 exploited zero-day vulnerabilities in 2023, 50% higher than in 2022. Chinese state actors, such as Volt Typhoon, were responsible for 12 of them, demonstrating their sophistication in discovering and exploiting unaddressed vulnerabilities. Enterprise software such as security software and networking is highlighted since it is a response to a change of strategy by attackers to target systems that are not equipped with endpoint detection and response (EDR) tools.

SonicWall executive director of threat research Douglas McKee put the importance of vulnerability research into perspective: "This attack serves as a reminder of how still unknown vulnerabilities can be used by professional attackers as a means of gaining access to critical infrastructure. Organizations must concentrate on third-party testing and internal security audits in order to stay one step ahead."

Global Implications and Recommendations

The bug has also raised alarms in high global cyber tensions, where strategic infrastructure is at the top of the priority list. US federal officials were alerted by Lumen Technologies in an effort to fortify against developing threats to strategic national assets. Cybersecurity professionals recommend that organizations actively take the following measures:

Patch Immediately: Patch to Versa Director version 22.1.4 or higher to fix CVE-2024-39717.

Network monitoring: Scan the user accounts, monitor logs, and rotate the credentials to detect inconsistencies.

Firewall Configuration: Apply relevant firewall rules to prevent exploitation of open management ports (4566 or 4570).

Threat Intelligence: Utilize anomaly-based detection and third-party threat feed to early detect zero-day attacks.

It is suggested by Mandiant researchers that if exploits for this vulnerability are published, in the guise of proof-of-concepts, other malicious operators will also utilize unpatched Versa Director servers, potentially expanding the extent of the attack.

The Race Against Zero-Days

Volt Typhoon campaign emphasizes the challenge of protecting against zero-day attacks, which leave an open window of opportunity for the bad guys until patches start to arrive. Although vendors like Versa Networks quickly leap at the opportunity to publish patches, hackers versus security experts' battle remains a long way from their demise. While Chinese hacking groups continue to be unchallenged in zero-day exploitation, global bodies must step up their cybersecurity stance in order to prevent such sneaky attacks.

For additional information on securing your systems, refer to Versa Networks' security advisory or reach out to CISA for guidance on mitigating known exploited vulnerabilities.

Sources: Black Lotus Labs, Lumen Technologies; Versa Networks; CISA; Google TAG and Mandiant
